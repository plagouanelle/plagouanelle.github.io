Short bio

I was born in 1995 in Toulouse (France) . After spending 20 years within a one-kilometre radius around my house with both highschool and CPGE in Lycée Pierre de Fermat (Toulouse, France), I entered the Ecole Normale Supérieure Paris-Saclay (formerly ENS Cachan) in 2015 in the departement of Physics. Research work

My work focuses on the uncertainty quantification for human exposure assessment on high-power transfer systems. Due to the high complexity of such systems, one cannot simply use a FEM model or conduct numerous experiments to perform an accurate sensitvity analysis using MCMC for example, as it would take too much computation time. teapplications of surrogate modelling on high-power inductive transfer systems.

Thus, I used a combination of Kriging and polynomial chaos expansion surrogate models called metamodelling, in order to build an accurate and consistent predictor for our complex models using fewer samples than classical methods. This predictor is an analytical function which can be computed at instant speed and allows fast and reliable sensitivity analysis. This sensitivity analysis can then be used to assess the behaviour of the system regarding human exposure guidelines. The accurate predictor can also be used instead of the estimated output model for optimization algorithm when building new designs.
